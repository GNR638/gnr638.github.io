Machine Learning for Remote Sensing - II

GNR638 - Spring 2026

Course Description

Remote sensing systems have become essential for understanding our planet, with applications in climate science, environmental monitoring, agriculture, urban planning, disaster response, and national security. This course teaches the principles and practical use of modern machine learning methods to address these challenges, and students will learn how deep learning architectures, including convolutional neural networks, recurrent networks, and Transformers are designed, trained, and adapted for both natural-world and remote sensing data. The course covers end-to-end learning for key tasks such as classification, object detection, segmentation, and image captioning, and introduces generative modeling techniques including GANs, VAEs, and diffusion models. It also teaches advanced learning paradigms such as domain adaptation, generalization, meta-learning, self-supervised learning, representation learning, and multimodal learning, which are critical for building robust models under limited labels and shifting data distributions. The course has both a theoretical and application-focused emphasis, and through case studies, students will gain the conceptual understanding and methodological tools needed to develop scalable and effective machine learning systems for remote sensing and related real-world applications.

Prerequisites

  • Proficiency in Python and C++ Class assignments for this course will be either in C++ or Python, with a particular focus on PyTorch, LibTorch, GPU programming, and related topics. Students are encouraged to review the provided resources to refresh and strengthen their skills in these programming languages.
  • Calculus and Linear Algebra Students should be comfortable with computing derivatives, performing dot products, and understanding key concepts such as eigenvectors and singular value decomposition (SVD). These skills will be critical for grasping advanced topics in machine learning and GPU programming.
  • Probability Students should have a good grasp of concepts in probability including topics such as the distributions, mean, variance, etc. and how it is applied in various machine learning and statistical methods.

Late Policy

  1. Total Late Days: Each student is allowed a total of 6 late days throughout the course. These can be used for assignments, blog writing, and project proposals.
  2. Task-Specific Limits: No more than 2 late days may be applied to any individual task.
  3. Project Submission Deadline: Late days cannot be used for project submissions. The project deadline is firm and cannot be extended.
  4. Submission After Late Days: Once the total allotted late days have been used, no further submissions will be accepted for any tasks.
  5. Deadlines: All deadlines will be set to 23:59 UTC, which corresponds to 05:30 IST the following day. For eg. deadline of 23:59 UTC on 26th January, 2026 would be 05:30 IST on 27th January, 2026.
  6. Group Activities: In the case of group activities, if any member of the group misses a deadline, all members of the group will be penalized.
  7. Peer Review Deadline: The peer review deadline is firm. Failure to complete the peer review on time will result in a penalty of 5 points from your final score.
  8. Medical or Personal Emergency: In the event of a medical or personal emergency that affects your ability to meet deadlines, please email your group's concerned TA, along with the professor and head TA as soon as possible. Appropriate accommodations or extensions may be discussed on a case-by-case basis.

Honour Code

In this course, students are encouraged to collaborate and discuss course material with peers, but all assignments must be completed independently. Using another student's work, copying, or submitting plagiarized material is strictly prohibited. Any external help, including assistance from AI tools, must be properly cited. Failing to adhere to these guidelines, including using uncredited help or engaging in dishonest practices, may result in disciplinary action as per the institute's policies. The course instructor reserves the right to assign a failing grade for the course or specific assignments if the honor code is violated.
